Edit. Recently I was curious about the origins and initial applications of the homological perturbation lemma (more on that below). As it turned out, it’s a beautiful story about the homology of simplicial fibrations that I had never heard of (due to (Shih 1962) and (Brown 1959)).

Conventions: We write \(C_*(X)=C_*(X, R)\) for the normalized chain complex of a simplicial set \(X\) with coefficients in a ring \(R\).

For spaces \(B\) and \(F\), the Eilenberg-Zilber theorem constucts chain equivalences \[\begin{xy} \xymatrix{ C_*(B\times F) \ar@(ur, ul)[r]^{} & C_*(B) \otimes C_*(F) \ar@(dl, dr)[l]^{} } \end{xy}\] computing the singular chains of the trivial bundle \(B \times F\) as a tensor product of the chains on \(B\) and the chains on \(F\). The twisted Eilenberg-Zilber theorem is the generalization to an arbitrary bundle \(E=B \times F\). We will work in the setting of simplicial sets instead of topological spaces. In this setting, the combinatorics of simplicial sets conspire to make Kan fibrations amenable to straightforward calculation. The key idea, due to (BGM?), is that of a minimal Kan fibration. We will not worry about the definition of minimality, but the following two results are important:

  • every Kan fibration fiberwise strong deformation retracts to a minimal Kan fibration

  • every minimal Kan fibration is a fiber bundle.

For details we refer to (GJ?) Proposition 10.3 and Corollary 10.7.

Theorem 1 (Twisted Eilenberg-Zilber). Let \(B\) and \(F\) be simplicial sets and let \(B \times_\tau F\) be a twisted Cartesian product. Then there is an equivalence \[C_*(B) \otimes_\tau C_*(F) \longrightarrow C_*(B \times_\tau F)\] where the complex on the left is the usual tensor product with a modified differential (the twisted tensor product (ebrown?)).

Brown, Edgar H., Jr. 1959. “Twisted Tensor Products. I.” Ann. Of Math. (2) 69: 223–46. https://doi.org/10.2307/1970101.
Shih, Weishu. 1962. “Homologie Des Espaces Fibrés.” Inst. Hautes Études Sci. Publ. Math., no. 13: 88.
